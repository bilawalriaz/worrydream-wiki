# [[jaynes-1990-scattering-of-light-by-free-electrons|Jaynes]] 1990 - Probability in Quantum Theory

## Core Thesis
[[jaynes-1990-scattering-of-light-by-free-electrons|Jaynes]] argues that the perceived fundamental difference between probability in quantum theory (QBism/Copenhagen interpretation) and probability in classical statistics or measurement error is a philosophical error, not a physical necessity. The orthodox view, as stated by Pauli, holds that [[caves-2001-quantum-probabilities-as-bayesian-probabilities|quantum probabilities]] represent a "principled indeterminacy" (a real, ontological randomness in Nature) rather than mere "lack of knowledge" (epistemic uncertainty). [[jaynes-1984-the-evolution-of-carnots-principle|Jaynes]] proposes a radical simplification: all probability signifies only incomplete human information. He explores the consequences of this unified Bayesian interpretation, suggesting it dissolves many quantum paradoxes. The paper's specific focus is on whether this viewpoint renders concepts like "zero-point energy" (a cornerstone of quantum field theory) as a real physical entity or merely as an artifact of an informational accounting error.

## Historical Context
The paper enters a debate that began with the foundational crisis of [[bush-2010-quantum-mechanics-writ-large|quantum mechanics]] in the 1920s-30s. The Copenhagen interpretation, championed by Bohr and Heisenberg, introduced a radical break: the act of measurement fundamentally disturbs a system, and probability is inherent in the theory, not due to ignorance. Einstein and Schrödinger resisted this, with Einstein's famous objection that "God does not play dice." By 1990, the debate had calcified. Multiple interpretations existed (Copenhagen, Many-Worlds, Pilot-Wave), but the mainstream considered the "measurement problem" solved or irrelevant to practice. [[jaynes-1984-the-evolution-of-carnots-principle|Jaynes]], a physicist and leading figure in Maximum Entropy methods for classical inference, saw the field as stuck in a rut of "smoky" dogma. He aimed to reopen the foundational discussion from a perspective rooted in probability theory as the logic of science, a view he attributed to Laplace and Bernoulli. He was countering the prevailing "compensation phenomenon" where "supreme self-confidence takes the place of rational arguments."

## Key Contributions
1.  **The Unified Information-Theoretic Thesis:** [[jaynes-1984-the-evolution-of-carnots-principle|Jaynes]] formally articulates the argument that quantum probability is epistemic, not ontological. This is not entirely new (similar to de Broglie-Bohm or early Quantum Bayesian ideas), but [[jaynes-1984-the-evolution-of-carnots-principle|Jaynes]] frames it as a direct application of standard probability theory to physics, challenging the need for a new "quantum logic."
2.  **Critique of the "Free Field" Quantization:** Using this thesis, he questions a standard QED procedure: separately quantizing the electromagnetic field (creating infinite degrees of freedom for a "free field") and then coupling it to matter. He suggests this introduces unnecessary infinities (zero-point energy, divergences) because the "free field" is an informational abstraction, not a necessary ontological entity. The real physics, he argues, resides in the source fields correlated with matter.
3.  **Re-interpretation of Zero-Point Energy:** He argues that zero-point energy, a direct consequence of quantizing the free field, may not be real energy that does work (e.g., contributing to the cosmological constant). Instead, it might be an artifact of the formalism—an "infinite uncertainty" assigned to initial conditions that doesn't produce observable dynamical effects.
4.  **Source-Field Theory as a Benchmark:** He highlights work in quantum optics that computes phenomena like spontaneous emission and the Lamb shift using only source fields (radiation reaction fields), suggesting the quantized free field is superfluous for these calculations. This provides a concrete, alternative technical framework.

## Methodology
The methodology is **theoretical and polemical**, structured as a philosophical critique followed by an exploration of consequences. [[jaynes-1984-the-evolution-of-carnots-principle|Jaynes]] does not present new empirical data. Instead, he:
*   **Contrasts Viewpoints:** Uses analogies (aether knots, Einstein on gravity/metric) to show how reframing a problem changes what seems mysterious.
*   **Applies Bayesian/Information Theory:** Leverages his expertise in probability as logic to argue for a consistent interpretation.
*   **Analyzes Formalism:** Examines the mathematical structure of QED (propagators, Hilbert spaces) to question whether certain quantized entities are functionally necessary.
*   **Seeks Consistency:** His core method is to follow the "unified view" of probability to see if it leads to contradictions or, as he claims, greater conceptual clarity.

## Influence
[[jaynes-1984-the-evolution-of-carnots-principle|Jaynes]]' paper was part of a small but persistent stream of thought that gained more traction in the 21st century with the rise of quantum information theory.
*   **Quantum Bayesianism (QBism):** [[jaynes-1984-the-evolution-of-carnots-principle|Jaynes]] is a direct precursor and intellectual ally of QBism, developed by Fuchs, [[mermin-1999-how-not-to-create-tigers|Mermin]], and others, which explicitly treats quantum states as states of an agent's knowledge.
*   **Quantum Information Foundations:** The paper's emphasis on information over ontology ali[[bush-2010-quantum-mechanics-writ-large|gns with the broa]]der shift towards viewing [[froehner-1993-quantum-mechanics-how-weird-for-bayesians|quantum mechanics]] through the lens of [[licklider-1968-interactive-information-processing-retrieval-and-transfer|information processing]].
*   **Continued Debate:** It fueled discussions in the foundations of physics community, cited in works exploring epistemic interpretations and the status of zero-point energy (e.g., in the context of the cosmological constant problem).
*   **Pedagogical Influence:** It became a key text for those skeptical of the "shut up and calculate" ethos, providing a rigorous, physical argument for re-examining interpretations.

*   **Quantum Computing & AI:** The success of quantum computing relies on manipulating information (qubits). [[jaynes-1984-the-evolution-of-carnots-principle|Jaynes]]' information-centric view provides a philosophical framework that aligns with this operational r[[licklider-1968-interactive-information-processing-retrieval-and-transfer|eality. *   **The Cosmological Constant Problem:** The absurdly large discrepancy between predicted and observed vacuum energy (zero-point energy) is one of the biggest problems in physics. [[jaynes-1984-the-evolution-of-carnots-principle|Jaynes]]' suggestion that zero-point energy is not "real" energy, but an artifact of quantization, is a perennially relevant perspective in this debate.
*   **Foundations of AI & Bayesian Reasoning:** [[jaynes-1984-the-evolution-of-carnots-principle|Jaynes]] was a foundational figure in applying Bayesian methods. His insistence on probability as the consistent logic of inference under uncertainty is central to modern machine learning and probabilistic AI. The paper extends this principle to the most fundamental level of physical theory, arguing for a coherent, unified theory of knowledge.
*   **Hyperflash's Work (Knowledge Management):** The paper's central question about the locus of information—whether in the modeler or the modeled—directly impacts how we design systems for knowledge capture and representation. [[jaynes-1984-the-evolution-of-carnots-principle|Jaynes]] cautions against reifying our models (like the "quantized free field") as autonomous realities, a warning for any system aiming to model complex domains.

## Key Quotes
1.  *"For some sixty years it has appeared to many physicists that probability plays a fundamentally different role in quantum theory than it does in statistical mechanics and analysis of measurement errors."*
    > **Analysis:** This sets up the paper's core conflict: the orthodoxy of a "two probability" universe versus [[jaynes-1984-the-evolution-of-carnots-principle|Jaynes]]' unified view. It frames the issue as a long-standing and widespread philosophical assumption within physics.
2.  *"All probability signifies only incomplete human information."*
    > **Analysis:** The paper's foundational axiom, stated bluntly. It rejects the Copenhagen view of quantum randomness as ontological, re-asserting the classical Bayesian interpretation of probability as a measure of ignorance.
3.  *"In this workshop we are venturing into a smoky area of science where nobody knows what the real truth is. Such fields are always dominated by the compensation phenomenon: supreme self-confidence takes the place of rational arguments."*
    > **Analysis:** A sharp, sociological observation about the dynamics of theoretical physics. [[jaynes-1984-the-evolution-of-carnots-principle|Jaynes]] argues that dogma fills the vacuum left by the absence of empirical proof in foundational debates, a call for more humility and rigorous argument.
4.  *"I feel that it is a delusion to think of the electrons and the fields as two physically different, independent entities."* (Quoting Einstein)
    > **Analysis:** [[jaynes-1984-the-evolution-of-carnots-principle|Jaynes]] uses Einstein's authority to attack the methodological separation of matter and field in QED. This isn't just about elegance; it's about whether the separate quantization procedure introduces physical and mathematical redundancies (infinities).
5.  *"To what extent does this information reside in us, and to wha[[vannevar-bush-symposium-1995-closing-panel|t ex]]tent is it a property of Nature?"*
    > [[bush-2010-quantum-mechanics-writ-large|**Analysis:** Thi]]s is the [[froehner-1993-quantum-mechanics-how-weird-for-bayesians|deepest, most end]]uring question in the paper. It transcends quantum mechanics and touches the philosophy of science, AI, and epistemology. It challenges the realism/anti-realism debate in a novel way, focused on information.
6.  *"The present EM field is a 'sufficient statistic' which merely summarizes all the information about past motion of charges that is relevant for predicting their future motion."*
    > **Analysis:** A key technical proposal. It redefines the electromagnetic field not as a fundamental physical entity, but as an optimal informational summary, potentially rendering the "free field" quantization unnecessary.
7.  *"One can hardly imagine a better way to generate infinities in physical predictions than by having a mathematical formalism with more degrees of freedom than are actually used by Nature."*
    > **Analysis:** A powerful, logically derived critique. If you put unnecessary complexity (infinite field modes) into your model, you should expect nonsensical outputs (infinities). The problem, [[jaynes-1984-the-evolution-of-carnots-principle|Jaynes]] implies, is in the model's construction, not in nature.
